# Hub Onboarding & Certification Process

## Overview

Qutlas manufacturers ("Hubs") undergo a structured certification process to ensure quality, reliability, and trustworthiness. This document outlines the requirements and steps.

## Certification Levels

### Basic Certification
- **Duration**: 2-3 weeks
- **Requirements**:
  - Valid business registration
  - Public liability insurance ($1M minimum)
  - Machine inventory documentation
  - Operator certifications (optional)
- **Benefits**: Listed on platform, access to leads, basic support
- **SLA**: Best effort basis

### Verified Certification
- **Duration**: 4-6 weeks (includes pilot phase)
- **Requirements**:
  - All Basic requirements +
  - Completion of 3 paid pilot jobs with ≥92% first-pass yield (FPY)
  - Quality management system documentation
  - Monthly uptime: ≥95%
- **Benefits**: Priority lead access, marketing support, financing options
- **SLA**: 99% uptime guaranteed, penalty clauses

### Premium Certification
- **Duration**: 8-12 weeks
- **Requirements**:
  - All Verified requirements +
  - Exclusive territory agreement
  - Dedicated account manager
  - Advanced telemetry and reporting
  - 12-month track record: FPY ≥95%, uptime ≥99%
- **Benefits**: Exclusive leads, pricing negotiation, co-marketing
- **SLA**: 99.9% uptime, white-glove support

## Application Process

### Step 1: Company Registration
Submit:
- Company name, legal entity
- Address and contact info
- Tax ID / Business registration number
- Primary contact email

### Step 2: Insurance & Compliance
Upload scanned copies:
- General liability insurance (≥$1M)
- Workers' compensation certificate
- Product liability (recommended)
- Operator certifications (ISO 9001, AS9100, etc.)

### Step 3: Machine Inventory
List all machines with:
- Type (CNC Mill, Laser, 3D Printer, etc.)
- Model and manufacturer
- Year acquired
- Condition (operational, maintenance, retired)
- Capabilities (axes, max speed, accuracy)

### Step 4: Network & Connectivity Test
- Qutlas provides a diagnostic tool
- Tests latency, bandwidth, and VPN connectivity
- Must achieve: <100ms latency, >5Mbps bandwidth
- Document results in dashboard

### Step 5: Training & Video Walkthrough
- Hub operator completes 30-min onboarding video
- Covers job acceptance workflow, telemetry, safety
- Recorded session stored for audit

### Step 6: Pilot Job Phase (Verified level only)
- System automatically routes 3 diverse jobs to hub
- Jobs cover different machine types and complexity
- Hub must achieve ≥92% first-pass yield per job
- Telemetry validated for accuracy and completeness

### Step 7: Admin Review & Approval
- Qutlas admin reviews all submitted materials
- May request clarifications
- Approval or rejection within 10 business days

## Ongoing Requirements

### Monthly Telemetry Reporting
- Cycle time statistics
- FPY and rejection rates
- Machine uptime logs
- Tool change frequency

### Quarterly Quality Audits
- Random sample inspection of completed parts
- Dimensional verification (CMM or caliper checks)
- Surface finish assessment
- Documentation review

### Annual Recertification
- Renewal of insurance certificates
- Updated machine inventory
- Review of telemetry trends
- Retraining for operators

## Decertification

A hub may lose certification if:
- Insurance lapses or drops below minimum
- Monthly uptime falls below SLA for >2 consecutive months
- FPY drops below 85% for >3 consecutive months
- Gross safety violation or customer complaint
- Non-payment of platform fees

Decertified hubs have 30 days to remediate or are permanently removed.
